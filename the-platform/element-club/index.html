Cfx Element Club.

The four subscription tiers that determine what a FiveM server can do. What each tier costs, what it unlocks, and what UK servers actually use in practice. Maintained as a permanent reference and updated whenever Cfx changes the tiers or their pricing.

Last updated · 19 April 2026 Review cadence · Quarterly Source · fivem.net/server-hosting

What Element Club is

Element Club is Cfx.re's paid subscription system for FiveM servers. In practical terms it is the mechanism by which Cfx funds platform development and server operators access capabilities that the base, free FiveM cannot deliver — principally, player slot counts above the default 48, plus a handful of cosmetic and streaming features.

Subscriptions are managed through the Cfx.re Portal at portal.cfx.re, which replaced the historic Patreon-based subscription path. A subscription is attached to a Cfx account and applies to all FiveM server licence keys registered under that account — operators running multiple servers on one account get the tier's benefits applied across all of them.

The four tiers

As of April 2026, Element Club offers a free baseline and three paid tiers. Pricing is in US dollars and excludes VAT; VAT is added at checkout for UK and EU subscribers. All figures sourced directly from fivem.net/server-hosting.

A note on pricing. Cfx lists Element Club tiers in US dollars. Sterling figures below are converted at approximately GBP/USD 1.35 for UK reader context; the exact amount charged to a UK card will vary with FX rates at the time of billing. Contributor programme

Cfx gives free Platinum access to active contributors — developers writing platform code, fixing bugs, improving documentation, or conducting game research. Application-based through the Cfx.re contributor programme. This is how some UK-scene developers run Platinum-tier servers without paying Platinum prices, and is worth knowing about for anyone publishing community work.

Why the tiers are really about OneSync

The headline differentiator between tiers is player slot count, but the underlying technology that makes those slot counts possible is called OneSync — Cfx's networking engine. Without OneSync, FiveM servers are limited to the base GTA Online player cap of 32.

OneSync has two modes. Standard OneSync provides moderate slot counts and is free at the baseline. OneSync Infinity is the high-performance mode required for meaningful scale, and is also free at the baseline tier since late 2020. What the paid tiers buy is the permission to raise the slot ceiling under OneSync Infinity — from 48 on the free tier up to 64, 128, or 2,048 depending on which tier is paid.

With the forthcoming FiveM for GTAV Enhanced release, OneSync is being overhauled. Cfx has stated that all Enhanced servers will run exclusively on the high-performance sync mode, and older lower-capacity modes are being dropped. Platinum remains required for 2,048-player servers under Enhanced. The full implications of the Enhanced migration are covered in this desk's Enhanced explainer.

What UK servers actually run

Systematic public data on what Element Club tier each UK server runs does not exist — Cfx does not publish it, and servers are under no obligation to disclose. What can be inferred from observable slot counts, however, tells a consistent story.

Any UK server running more than 48 concurrent players is paying for Element Club at Argentum or above. Any server with a reported capacity above 128 is running Platinum. Serious UK RP servers with 250, 500, or 1,000+ player populations on their most active nights are, by definition, running Platinum at £37 per month. A year of Platinum is £444. A server with three years of operational history on Platinum has spent upwards of £1,333 on Element Club alone — before hosting costs, framework licences, or the commercial scripts increasingly sold through the Cfx Marketplace.

The editorial observation worth making here, and one this desk returns to frequently, is that the framing of "community-owned" or "community-run" that many UK servers use in their public presentation sits uneasily with Platinum subscription costs of this scale. Community labour runs the servers; community donations often fund the subscriptions. But the commercial infrastructure is unmistakably commercial. It is worth the scene being honest about this — the best servers are better described as small businesses with community participation than as community projects with incidental costs.

How to subscribe

  • Step one. Visit the Element Club page on the Cfx.re Portal at portal.cfx.re/subscriptions/element-club.
  • Step two. Sign in with the same Cfx account that holds the FiveM server licence keys. If subscriptions and keys are on different accounts, the benefits will not link.
  • Step three. Select the tier, enter payment details (Tebex-processed), and complete the purchase.
  • Step four. Benefits are typically active within two to six hours. If keys still show the prior tier after six hours, contact Cfx support through the Portal.

Tier upgrades and downgrades

Upgrades (e.g. Argentum to Aurum) take effect immediately. The operator pays the pro-rated difference between the old and new tiers and benefits update within hours.

Downgrades (e.g. Platinum to Argentum) take effect only at the next renewal date. The operator retains the higher tier's benefits until the current billing period ends, then drops to the new tier. A cancelled subscription must be allowed to expire fully before a new one can be started.

This asymmetry — instant upgrades, renewal-delayed downgrades — is a standard commercial pattern designed to capture upgrade revenue while preventing mid-month credit losses. Operators budgeting around Element Club should plan accordingly.

What Element Club does not include

Three clarifications worth stating explicitly because each is regularly confused in community discussion.

Element Club is not server hosting. It does not provide the physical server, bandwidth, or administrative tooling that actually runs a FiveM server. Those come from an authorised hosting provider (Zap-Hosting, EUGameHost, and others are commonly used in the UK) or from the operator's own infrastructure. Element Club is a software licence tier; hosting is a separate cost.

Element Club is not Tebex. Tebex is FiveM's exclusive monetisation partner and handles server stores for paying communities. Tebex revenue is entirely separate from Element Club subscription spend. Servers that monetise through Tebex pay Tebex's fees on community sales, on top of whatever Element Club tier they run.

Element Club is not the Cfx Marketplace. The Marketplace, launched in January 2026, is a Rockstar-Cfx storefront selling paid mods to server operators. Element Club is the underlying server subscription. They are distinct commercial products on the same commercial platform (both run on Tebex as merchant of record).

Changes expected with FiveM Enhanced

Cfx has not published any tier or pricing changes tied to the forthcoming FiveM for GTAV Enhanced release. The March 2026 development update confirmed that Platinum remains required for 2,048-player servers under Enhanced. Whether sub-Platinum tiers unlock the same features on Enhanced as on Legacy — particularly the exact slot counts associated with Argentum and Aurum — has not been explicitly addressed by Cfx and is one of the outstanding questions this desk is tracking ahead of the Enhanced early-access rollout.

This reference page will be updated when Cfx publishes explicit Enhanced-tier guidance. Until then, operators planning migrations should assume tier benefits carry over and budget accordingly, but treat the assumption as provisional.
