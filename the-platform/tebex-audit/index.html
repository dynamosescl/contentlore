The Tebex Audit.

A systematic quarterly audit of every UK FiveM server's Tebex store. What is being sold, at what price, against what marketing, and in what relationship with Rockstar's Platform License Agreement. Published with right-of-reply, evidence, and editorial restraint.

Methodology published · 19 April 2026 First audit · First week of May 2026 Cadence · Quarterly thereafter

Why this audit exists

Every serious UK FiveM roleplay server runs a Tebex store. Some of those stores are modest: a single priority queue tier, a donation option, a few cosmetic extras. Others are substantially more commercial: multi-tier subscription ladders, exclusive vehicle packs, priority whitelist access, paid RP advantages that sit in ambiguous relationship with Rockstar's Platform License Agreement. In aggregate, Tebex is how the UK FiveM scene makes money. Nobody systematically documents that.

The Tebex Audit is The Platform's answer to that gap. Quarterly, this desk will survey every UK server with a public Tebex store, record what each is selling and at what prices, and publish the results as a structured, ranked, sourced report. Where we see evidence of PLA-ambiguous practices, we flag them under a defined scoring framework and send right-of-reply to the affected server before publication. The goal is not to embarrass operators; it is to make the commercial shape of the UK scene legible for readers who cannot otherwise see it.

What we audit

Each audit covers five dimensions per server. Every dimension is scored on a 1-to-5 scale with the same methodology applied across every server in the audit, to produce a ranked leaderboard alongside the per-server detail. The five dimensions:

01

Catalogue breadth

How many distinct products, at what price range, across what categories. A wider catalogue is not automatically worse, but catalogue shape tells us how a server thinks about monetisation.

02

Pricing posture

Entry-tier price, top-tier price, and the curve between them. How aggressive is the pricing ladder? What does the top tier actually unlock?

03

PLA alignment

The most editorially sensitive dimension. Rockstar's PLA prohibits selling real advantages in-game. Where store listings suggest otherwise, we flag them with evidence and send right-of-reply.

04

Marketing honesty

Does the store's product copy match the server's public marketing? Where a server claims to be community-owned but sells commercial tiers that say otherwise, we note the gap.

05

Transparency

Does the server publicly explain where the money goes? Revenue transparency is not obligatory but is a community trust signal worth scoring. Servers that disclose score higher than servers that do not.

Right-of-reply, always

Every server that receives a flag on any dimension is contacted privately before publication. The communication includes the specific evidence supporting the flag — screenshots, URLs, dated observations — and invites the operator to respond. The operator's response, if substantive, is published alongside the flag. If the operator provides context that changes the editorial reading, the flag is revised or removed before publication. If the operator declines to respond or provides no substantive reply, the flag is published with a note that right-of-reply was offered and not accepted.

This is not a discretionary kindness; it is a professional obligation and a protection for both the servers and this publication. Public coverage of commercial practices without right-of-reply is neither ethically sound nor legally robust. Every Tebex Audit piece is published to a standard that would survive a Defamation Act 2013 serious harm challenge — which means evidenced, contextualised, offered for reply, and written in restrained language.

Editorial principles

Five commitments govern every piece of Tebex Audit coverage, stated explicitly here so they cannot be treated as private judgement calls later.

  • Evidence is preserved. Every flag is supported by a timestamped screenshot or equivalent archived record of the source material. Claims that cannot be evidenced are not made.
  • Language is restrained. The difference between reporting "under review" and calling something "fraudulent" is a legal and editorial one. This desk uses the former even where the latter feels earned, until the evidence is beyond challenge.
  • Servers get right-of-reply on every flag, every time. No exceptions, no surprise publications, no gotcha reporting.
  • Operators get corrections. Where a reader or operator demonstrates an error in published coverage, the correction is made promptly and logged visibly on the piece.
  • The methodology is public. The scoring approach documented on this page applies to every server in every audit. Servers cannot be graded by standards that only exist after the fact.

The schedule

The Tebex Audit is quarterly. Each audit covers the preceding three months' observations, with data collection running continuously through the quarter and the published report landing in the first week of the quarter following.

Why Tebex specifically

Tebex is FiveM's exclusive monetisation partner. Every UK server that accepts money from its community through a standard sanctioned path does so through Tebex. That exclusivity is now more significant, not less, since the Cfx Marketplace — Rockstar's first-party mod storefront — also runs on Tebex as merchant of record. Tebex is, effectively, the commercial spine of the entire FiveM ecosystem. Auditing its UK usage is auditing the UK scene's commercial shape.

Servers that accept payment outside Tebex — through private PayPal arrangements, direct Discord payments, or other non-sanctioned channels — sit outside this audit's scope. This is not because those channels do not matter. It is because they are not publicly observable in a way that supports systematic audit methodology. Where scene reporting surfaces credible evidence of non-Tebex commercial activity, that will be covered separately under Platform dispatches rather than folded into the audit's structured data.

What this audit is not

Three scope exclusions worth stating before the inaugural audit lands, because each of them is predictably misinterpreted.

It is not an enforcement action. This desk has no authority to sanction, penalise, or force change at any server. Where flags are published, they are editorial observations with evidence, not legal determinations. The only sanction a Tebex Audit flag carries is the editorial one: it exists in public, under a permanent URL, citable by others.

It is not an anti-monetisation project. This desk has written in the Marketplace at 100 days piece that running a serious UK RP server is running a business, and that a community at scale requires commercial infrastructure to sustain. The audit is not arguing against monetisation. It is arguing for legibility about what monetisation looks like, so players and community members can make informed decisions about where they spend.

It is not a one-off piece of coverage. The Tebex Audit is a permanent, quarterly commitment of this desk. The inaugural audit in May is the first of many. A server whose practices change between audits will see those changes reflected in the next cycle.

A note to UK server operators

If you operate a UK FiveM server with a public Tebex store, your store will be in the inaugural audit. If your store will show any flag on any scoring dimension, you will receive right-of-reply at least ten days before publication via the contact method publicly listed on your Cfx forum account, your server website, or your Discord. If none of those channels is publicly discoverable, this desk will attempt discovery through scene contacts before publishing.

The most useful thing an operator can do now, ahead of the May audit, is make sure the server's public contact is current. The least useful is to change store practices between now and the audit specifically to avoid flags. That pattern is observable in quarter-on-quarter change detection from Q3 onward; better to make considered long-term changes than cosmetic pre-audit ones.

The Tebex Audit's first data point is in preparation now. The published report lands the first week of May.
