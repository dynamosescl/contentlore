The Ledger.

ContentLore's public record of corrections, updates, retractions, and editorial disclosures. Every factual error corrected on a published piece appears here, permanently, with the date, the affected piece, and what changed. Every material conflict of interest, editorial judgement, or procedural decision worth disclosing is logged here too.

What the Ledger is

The Ledger is this publication's accountability mechanism in public form. It exists on the principle that a publication that is willing to identify its mistakes, and to keep the record of those mistakes visible permanently, is more credible than one that quietly revises pages when errors are found. Every correction on ContentLore is logged here. No correction is ever removed from the Ledger, even when the underlying piece is unpublished, because the record of the correction is itself part of the public accountability.

The Ledger also records editorial decisions that are worth disclosing even when they are not corrections — material conflicts of interest, scene-knowledge overrides that contradict public sources, pieces that are held or modified for reasons readers should know about. A fully transparent Ledger is more useful than a minimal one.

Entry types

Correction

A factual error being fixed.

A published claim that is demonstrably incorrect, evidenced by source material, corrected promptly and logged permanently here. The piece itself is updated with a correction notice at the top.

Update

New information added.

Material additions to a piece as new information becomes available. Not a correction — the original was not wrong — but substantial enough to be logged for transparency.

Disclosure

An editorial decision or COI.

Conflicts of interest, scene-knowledge overrides, methodology changes, or other editorial decisions worth stating publicly so readers can calibrate trust accordingly.

Retraction

A piece withdrawn entirely.

Reserved for cases where a published piece has errors severe enough that no correction can rescue it. The piece is unpublished, the reason is logged, and the Ledger entry remains permanently.

Current log

Entries are ordered with the most recent at the top. The log is append-only; entries are never removed, even when the underlying situation resolves.

Unique RP editorial relationship — material COI logged.

How entries are triggered

A Ledger entry is created when any one of the following is true.

Reader-submitted corrections

A reader, a subject of coverage, or any third party identifies a factual error in a published piece and the error is confirmed. The reader submission process is documented on the Contact page. Submissions are acknowledged within forty-eight hours and, where the correction is confirmed, logged within twenty-four hours of confirmation.

Desk-identified corrections

This desk identifies its own error — through subsequent reporting, source follow-up, or reading the piece back with fresh eyes. Where a factual error is confirmed, the Ledger entry is created immediately and the piece is updated in parallel.

Updates to evolving stories

A published piece covers a situation that has since developed, and the new information is material enough that a reader of the original piece should know about it. Updates are logged only when they are substantive; minor clarifications or cosmetic edits are handled in-piece without Ledger entry.

Editorial disclosures

Conflicts of interest, methodology changes, scene-judgement overrides, and other editorial decisions worth stating publicly. These are logged proactively, without waiting for an external party to raise them.

What is not in the Ledger

Some things that readers might expect to find here, which deliberately are not.

Minor copy edits. Fixing a typo, clarifying an ambiguous phrase, or refining prose without changing factual claims does not trigger a Ledger entry. The piece itself is updated, without a correction notice, because no claim has materially changed.

Style changes. Changes to design, navigation, or site structure that do not affect editorial claims are not Ledger entries. They are logged separately in site versioning.

Private source protections. Where a piece uses anonymous sources, the Ledger does not disclose those sources retrospectively even if the underlying story becomes better-documented publicly. Source protection is maintained indefinitely, because retrospective unmasking would undermine every future source's confidence in this publication's commitment.

Opinions a reader disagrees with. The Ledger is for factual corrections, not interpretive ones. A reader who thinks this publication's framing of a development is wrong has an opinion, not a correction. Those responses are welcomed through the Contact channels but do not trigger Ledger entries unless the underlying fact being interpreted is itself incorrect.

Historical versions

Where a piece has been substantively corrected, the original version remains accessible on request. The published version always reflects the current accurate state, but this publication retains prior versions of every piece for transparency and accountability. Requests for historical versions can be made through the Contact page.

Historical versions of this standards document and the Ledger itself are retained indefinitely. Version tracking on editorial standards is visible on the Editorial standards page at the top of the document.
