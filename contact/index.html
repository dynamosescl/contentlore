Contact · Five channels · Different purposes

How to reach us.

Five separate channels for five separate purposes. Use the one that matches what you are getting in touch about — we respond faster when messages arrive on the right channel, and some channels carry confidentiality commitments that only apply when the correct route is used.

The channels below are ordered by purpose, not priority — all are read and responded to. Each has its own address, its own response-time target, and its own rules about confidentiality. Please read the channel description before sending to make sure you are using the right route for your message.

Channel 01
For people inside the scene with information
Response · Within 48 hours

Scene tips

If you are a server operator, a creator, a community member, or anyone else with information about something happening in the UK streaming or FiveM scene that you think should be covered, this is the channel. We accept tips confidentially or on-the-record — that is your call, and we will honour whichever you choose.

Good tips include: scene movements not yet public, commercial developments on servers or platforms, creator transitions, policy changes, platform decisions. Less useful: personal drama without structural significance, disputes between individuals where no wider story exists, complaints about coverage (for which the corrections channel is the right route).

If you request confidentiality, your identity is protected indefinitely. This publication does not reveal anonymous sources retrospectively under any circumstances. If we cannot use your tip without burning your confidentiality, we will discuss that with you before publication, not afterwards.

Send to
tips@contentlore.com
Channel 02
For subjects of coverage responding to claims
Response · Same day where possible, 24 hours maximum

Right-of-reply

If you are the subject of a ContentLore piece — named as an individual, server, or organisation — and you want to respond to the claims being made, this is the channel. Right-of-reply is a standing commitment of this publication, documented in full in the Editorial standards.

Right-of-reply submissions are handled quickly because they usually concern time-sensitive publication decisions. If a piece naming you is pending, your response is read before the piece goes live, and substantive responses are published alongside the piece. If a piece naming you is already live, a substantive response is added to the piece promptly and logged in the Ledger.

For the Tebex Audit specifically: right-of-reply windows open ten days before publication, and if you are receiving a flag, you are contacted directly in advance. You do not need to use this channel unless your contact details have changed since we last had them.

Send to
reply@contentlore.com
Channel 03
For anyone identifying a factual error
Response · Within 48 hours · Correction within 24 hours of confirmation

Corrections

If you have identified a factual error in a ContentLore piece — a wrong date, a misquoted source, an incorrect claim about a person or organisation, a miscalculated figure — this is the channel. Corrections are the highest-priority class of reader contact we receive; the credibility of every piece on this site depends on errors being identified and fixed promptly.

Please include, where you can: the piece URL, the specific claim that is incorrect, the evidence supporting your correction (a link, a screenshot, a public source), and your relationship to the matter if you have one (for example, if you are the subject being incorrectly described). None of these is mandatory, but they all help us process your correction quickly.

Confirmed corrections are logged in the Ledger with date, affected piece, and nature of the correction. The piece itself is updated with a correction notice at the top.

Send to
corrections@contentlore.com
Channel 04
For journalists, researchers, and external media
Response · Within one week

Press and research

If you are a journalist, academic researcher, industry analyst, or external media professional looking to use ContentLore coverage in your own work, ask about the scene, commission contribution, or arrange an interview with this publication, this is the channel.

Coverage on this site is quotable under standard journalism practice — attribution to ContentLore, link where possible, and a courtesy note before publication is always appreciated but not required. Data and analysis produced by this publication (including the Scoreboard and the Tebex Audit) are available for citation under the same terms.

For more involved collaboration — guest contribution, data licensing, or syndication arrangements — please describe what you are looking for and we will respond with what is practical.

Send to
press@contentlore.com
Channel 05
For commercial approaches
Policy · Declined by default

Partnerships, sponsorship, and PR

ContentLore does not accept sponsored coverage, paid-for features, advertising placements, affiliate relationships with covered entities, commissioned content from subjects of coverage, or any other arrangement where money flows from covered subjects to this publication. This is a structural commitment, documented in the Editorial standards, not a pricing discussion.

If you are a PR professional pitching on behalf of a creator, server, or brand, the most useful thing you can do is send the underlying information to the tips channel (Channel 01). We will evaluate coverage on editorial merit. Pitches that include offers of payment, affiliate deals, or exchange-for-coverage arrangements are not responded to.

If you believe your approach is genuinely different from standard sponsored coverage — for example, you are a tooling or infrastructure provider proposing a non-editorial technical partnership — please explain clearly what you are offering and what you are asking for. These are rare and evaluated case-by-case, but the default answer remains no.

Send to (if necessary)
business@contentlore.com

Commitments across all channels

What we commit to

  • Acknowledgement. Every message on every channel is acknowledged. If you send to ContentLore and hear nothing within the channel's SLA window, it means we did not receive the message — please resend.
  • Confidentiality where promised. If a channel carries confidentiality (tips, right-of-reply on sensitive matters), that confidentiality is absolute and indefinite. No source is ever identified retrospectively.
  • No surveillance. This publication does not use analytics tools that identify individual readers, does not track which pieces specific IPs visit, and does not share reader data with any third party. Contact email addresses are used only to respond to the message and any direct follow-up with you.
  • No reader mailing list from Contact submissions. Messages sent to any of the above channels do not sign you up for anything. Newsletter subscription is a separate, opt-in process on the Rising page.
  • Data retention. Contact correspondence is retained only for as long as necessary to handle your message and any follow-up. Archived messages are destroyed after twelve months unless you have requested otherwise or the correspondence relates to ongoing editorial matters.

What not to send

Three categories of message that waste your time and ours.

General feedback or complaints about coverage framing that are not factual corrections. Opinions about a piece's emphasis, tone, or interpretive angle are welcomed — send them to the press channel — but they are not corrections and will not trigger a Ledger entry. If you think a piece is factually wrong, the corrections channel is the right route; if you think a piece is framed in a way you disagree with, press is.

Requests to remove pieces because a subject does not like coverage of them. Pieces are written to meet editorial standards, not to please their subjects. Right-of-reply exists to offer a published response to coverage; it is not a mechanism for removing coverage that has met standards. Retraction is reserved for pieces that fail those standards, not pieces that land accurately but uncomfortably.

Bulk pitches, templated PR, and AI-generated outreach. We read every message but we do not respond to pitches that appear to have been sent to ten other publications at the same time. Specificity earns attention; bulk outreach does not.
